# The x402 Ecosystem: 311 Companies, 3 Signals, 1 Dataset

x402 is the HTTP-native payment protocol for AI agents — a standard that lets any agent pay for any API, dataset, or service with a single line of code and no accounts, no API keys, no OAuth flows. Coinbase shipped it. Developers are building on it fast.

We've been tracking every company that's submitted a PR to the x402 ecosystem registry at `github.com/coinbase/x402`. As of March 2026, that's **311 companies** across payments, infrastructure, data, AI tooling, and DeFi. This is Double Agent — and this is what we found.

## The Three Signals

We enrich every entry with three agent-readiness signals:

**`agent-card.json`** — Does the company expose an A2A-compliant agent card? This tells other agents what the service does, how to call it, and what it costs. Companies with this are actively building for agent-to-agent interaction.

**`llms.txt`** — Does the company have an `llms.txt` at their domain? This is the emerging standard for making web content machine-readable. Having one signals that the team is thinking about AI-native interfaces.

**`resolved.sh`** — Is the company registered on resolved.sh, the agent commerce directory? This means they're not just building for agents — they're selling to them.

Of 311 companies tracked, a meaningful subset has at least one signal. A small group has all three.
